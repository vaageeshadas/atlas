The power in Japan transitioned from the imperial house to great clans over centuries, eventually leading to the rise of military clans and samurai. The Minamoto clan emerged victorious in the Genpei War and established the Kamakura shogunate. After facing two Mongol invasions, the shogunate was eventually overthrown in 1333, leading to the Muromachi period. Daimy\u014d rose in power during this period, leading to civil wars. Oda Nobunaga and Toyotomi Hideyoshi reunified Japan before Tokugawa Ieyasu became shogun and established the Tokugawa shogunate during the Edo period. Contact with Portugal in 1543 led to the introduction of firearms and later opening up of Japan in the Meiji era. Japan experienced rapid industrialization and modernization, leading to participation in World War I and territorial expansion. The Taish\u014d era saw democratic developments, literary growth, and economic growth, culminating in the reign of Emperor Akihito. The post World War II era was marked by economic challenges and social issues, with ongoing tensions due to Japan's wartime legacy. Gender equality fluctuated over centuries, with women's rights gradually improving over time.
